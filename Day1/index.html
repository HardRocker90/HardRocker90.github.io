Fun facts about Curitiba

  • It was originally called Vila da Nossa Senhora da Luz dos Pinhais, which literally translates as 'Our Lady of the Light in the Pine Forest'. It was renamed Curitiba in the year of 1721, and was officially listed as a town only a century later, in 1812.
  • Known for it's green areas and urban planning, the city holds the titles of Ecological capital of Brazil, and Most environmentally sustainable city in South America
  • Curitiba is the coldest capital in our country. In winter, the temperature can easily reach 0ºC (32ºF). There are snow records, and the lowest ever was -6ºC (-21.2ºF)
  • The city's population is 1.933.105 (one million nine hundred thirty-three thousand one hundred five) people, according to our last census
